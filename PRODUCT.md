# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

A general internet audience — people who land here from a shared link with zero context, in curiosity mode rather than task mode. Their job is not to _use_ anything; it is to experience the front page and, if it takes hold, drift deeper. The author is the second user: the keeper of the archive, logging dreams over time.

## Product Purpose

An art project: a public log of the author's real dreams, each entry paired with an AI-generated image made from it. There is no product underneath — the design is the product. Success is a beautiful personal archive: an artifact the author is proud to keep adding to, that rewards anyone who wanders in.

## Positioning

This is what dreams actually look like — a real, ongoing dream diary rendered into images, not a gallery about dreams but a place that behaves like one.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: primary — experience the front page itself; the landing is the artwork. Secondary — drift deeper into individual dream entries.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "This is what dreams actually look like."
- Belief ladder: (1) these are real dreams, logged by a real person; (2) each image was generated from that dream; (3) this is worth drifting through, and worth coming back to.
- Proof on hand: not applicable — the dreams and their dated, ongoing accumulation are the proof.

## Brand Personality

Eerie, cinematic, mysterious. A visitor should feel dreamlike wonder: surreal, floating, slightly disoriented — like being inside a dream rather than reading about one. The voice is first-person and quiet, a diary spoken aloud; the atmosphere carries the strangeness so the words don't have to strain for it.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing page: hero + features + CTA scaffolding, gradient accents, card grids.
- Instagram-style image grid: a uniform thumbnail wall that flattens every dream into interchangeable content.
- Goth / horror kitsch: eerie is not spooky — no fog overlays, dripping fonts, or purple-black clichés.
- Minimal portfolio template: the tasteful-but-anonymous white page with small gray captions.

No existing site is the reference. The register of dreams themselves is the only guide — invent the look rather than borrowing a lane.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is the artwork.** Every screen is the exhibit, not a container for one. If a section could be lifted onto another site unchanged, it isn't done.
2. **Dream logic over web logic.** Pacing, navigation, and composition may disorient by design; wonder beats efficiency. The visitor should feel led, not routed.
3. **Eerie, never spooky.** The uncanny comes from restraint, atmosphere, and what's withheld — never from horror props.
4. **Each dream is its own world.** Entries are not uniform content units; resist any structure that flattens them into a grid of equals.
5. **A real diary, honestly kept.** Dates, gaps, and imperfections are part of the piece — it is an ongoing ritual, not a curated demo.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: all text meets AA contrast against its actual background, including text over imagery. Every animation has a genuine `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative — the piece must still work as a quiet, still artifact. Alt text is written as part of the voice, describing each dream image the way the diary would.
